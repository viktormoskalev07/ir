Improving people’s lives with the power of empathy

Our technology establishes long-term relationships between people and technology, unlocking unprecedented continuous engagement. We’re using this breakthrough to help people live better, starting with older adults.

Milestones

2016
Founded

Intuition Robotics was founded in 2016 by our 3 Co-Founders: Dor Skuler, Roy Amir, and Itai Mendelsohn.

2016
Seed round

To fuel our growth, we secured our first-ever $2M in funding.

2017
ElliQ Launch and Induction at Design Museum

Intuition Robotics was founded in 2016 by our 3 Co-Founders: Dor Skuler, Roy Amir, and Itai Mendelsohn.

2017
Series A

We were fortunate to secure $14M Series A, and began expanding our team even more.

2018
Beta testing journey begins

In order to validate ElliQ’s capabilities, we began our beta testing process in older adults’ homes throughout the US. Their feedback played an invaluable role in shaping the product into what it is today.

2019
Design win with Toyota

Our cognitive AI platform was selected to power Yui, Toyota’s empathetic digital companion for their LQ concept car.

2019
10k days in older adults’ homes

As our beta testing process continued, ElliQ units collectively spent over 10,000 days in older adults’ homes. Over time, many have formed a meaningful bond with ElliQ, and turn to ElliQ as a genuine source of comfort and support.

2020
Series B

Just before the pandemic struck, we secured our $36M Series B Funding Round, bringing our total funding to $58M.

2020
Fighting the pandemic blues

Due to heightened loneliness and social isolation amid Covid-19, we launched a free program across the US, to support older adults via ElliQ as they sheltered in place.

2021
30k days ElliQ in older adults’ homes

Through new initiatives and partnerships, we continued to expand ElliQ’s presence into as many older adults’ homes as possible. Some older adults have lived with ElliQ for an extended period of time — some for over 2 years.

2021
Healthcare launch

Our cognitive AI platform was selected to power Yui, Toyota’s empathetic digital companion for their LQ concept car.
